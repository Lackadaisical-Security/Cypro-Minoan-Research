The decipherment approach built on five prior phases of intensive analysis, each contributing a layer of certainty. In Phase 1, a comprehensive sign inventory and classification was completed. ## Phonetic Values and Transliteration System

The derived phonetic values of Cypro-Minoan signs are grounded in their Linear A/B correspondences and cross-script analysis. In our transliteration, we use lowercase Latin letters a, e, i, o, u for vowels, and conventionally approximate consonants by their Linear B equivalents (e.g. t, p, k, m, n, r, s, d, g, y, w, h). For example, a sign that in Linear B is pa (ğ€) is given the transliteration pa in Cypro-Minoan if the shape matches and it fits the pattern distribution (and indeed one Cypro-Minoan sign closely resembles Linear A/B PA and occurs in similar contexts). However, until actual sound values are confirmed via a known language, these assignments are considered phonetic approximations. We include [IPA] International Phonetic Alphabet suggestions for reconstructed sounds in lexicon entries where possible, but note these are tentative. The language likely had sounds not identical to Mycenaean Greek; for instance, what we transliterate as r might have been a trill [r] or a lateral [l] in reality (Linear scripts typically do not distinguish R/L). We have found evidence for at least five vowel qualities (a, e, i, o, u) and around twelve consonantal phonemes, including a probable /k/ (or /g/), /t/ (or /d/), /p/ (or /b/), /m/, /n/, /s/, /y/ (j), /w/, and possibly a /kh/ or similar rough breathing (as some words appear to begin with an h-like sound in comparative analysis).
